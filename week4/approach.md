# Week 4 Approach

Week 4 continues the late-stage trust-region strategy rather than introducing a new global search method. After three rounds of observations, the strongest signal is that most functions now have a credible basin, and the remaining query budget should be spent refining those basins rather than discovering entirely new regions.

The operating rule for Week 4 is:

- hard exploit where the latest round produced a new best
- soft exploit where the current basin is strong but not yet improving
- cautious local refinement where the signal is improving but still below the historical best
- reset toward the best historical basin where raw model proposals drift too far away

The raw Week 4 candidates were generated by the trust-region script, but the final submission remains a blended set. Gaussian-process candidates for the lower-dimensional functions were still too jumpy, and even the stronger higher-dimensional random-forest candidates benefited from sanity checks before being accepted unchanged.

The core checks used for Week 4 were:

- distance from the best historical point
- trust-region adherence
- nearest-neighbour outcome quality
- boundary behaviour
- classifier-based region support using logistic regression and an RBF SVM

The decision rule did not change after adding the classifiers. The classifier outputs were useful supporting evidence, especially for Functions 5 to 8, but they did not replace the geometric and basin-aware sanity checks. The main lesson from the checks is that the late-stage strategy still works: stay local, avoid unjustified basin jumps, and manually constrain boundary-seeking proposals.
